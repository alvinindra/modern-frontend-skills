  1. 01

    The cloth comes in by hand.

    We buy our cotton from a mill in Tewkesbury that has been weaving for our family since 1962. The flax travels by container ship from Kortrijk. We accept nothing pre-treated.

    3-week average lead time
  2. 02

    It is drawn full-size on the loft floor.

    Every sail begins in chalk on Beech planks. We measure the boat in the water, not on paper. A mainsail for a 33-foot hull takes two days to lay before we touch the cloth.

    2 days of layout
  3. 03

    It is cut, broad-seamed, and stitched.

    One person on each sail, start to finish. Heavy corners are hand-stitched in waxed Dacron thread. Bronze grommets are set with the same press my grandfather paid forty dollars for at a Providence auction.

    11–14 days on the bench
  4. 04

    It goes back out, and we follow it.

    Every new sail is sea-trialled with the owner. We sail with you the first afternoon. Anything we can fix that week, we will. We have not missed a delivery sail since 2003.

    1 afternoon of sea trial
